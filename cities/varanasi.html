The Eternal City

Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Benaras, is one of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities and the spiritual capital of India.

Spiritual Significance

Varanasi is considered the holiest of the seven sacred cities in Hinduism. Devotees believe that dying here brings salvation (moksha).

Historical Heritage

With over 3,000 years of history, Varanasi has been a cultural and religious hub since ancient times, mentioned in the Rigveda.

Cultural Center

Varanasi is renowned for its classical music, silk weaving, and vibrant festivals that bring the city to life with colors and devotion.

Experience Dev Deepawali in Varanasi

Witness the "Festival of Gods" when over a million earthen lamps illuminate the ghats of Varanasi, creating a celestial spectacle on the banks of the Ganges.

Celebrated on the full moon of Kartik month (November), Dev Deepawali transforms the city into a shimmering paradise with cultural performances and rituals.

Travel Tips

Make the most of your spiritual journey to Varanasi

Best Time to Visit

October to March offers pleasant weather (10°C to 25°C). For festival experiences, visit during Dev Deepawali (November) or Maha Shivratri (February/March). Avoid summer months (April-June) with temperatures up to 45°C.

Getting There

Varanasi has an international airport (VNS) with connections to major cities. The city is well-connected by rail (Varanasi Junction) and road. Auto-rickshaws and cycle rickshaws are common for local transport.

Temple Etiquette

Dress modestly (covered shoulders and knees), remove footwear before entering temples, and avoid photography in inner sanctums. Many temples have strict security checks.

Ghat Exploration

Take an early morning boat ride to see rituals at the ghats. Be respectful during cremation ceremonies at Manikarnika Ghat - photography is strictly prohibited.
