Land of High Passes

Leh Ladakh, India's highest plateau, is renowned for its stark mountain landscapes, turquoise lakes, and ancient Buddhist culture.

Mountain Landscapes

Home to the world's highest motorable roads, Ladakh offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas and Karakoram ranges.

Buddhist Heritage

Centuries-old monasteries like Hemis, Thiksey, and Diskit preserve ancient traditions and Tibetan Buddhist culture.

Adventure Activities

From trekking to river rafting, Ladakh offers thrilling experiences for adventure enthusiasts.

Travel Tips

Essential information for your Ladakh journey

Best Time to Visit

June-September: Pleasant weather (15°C-30°C) with all roads and passes open.
October-November: Cooler with fewer crowds, beautiful autumn colors.
December-February: Extreme cold (-20°C) but stunning frozen landscapes.

Permit Requirements

Inner Line Permit (ILP) required for domestic tourists to visit protected areas.
Protected Area Permit (PAP) needed for foreign nationals.
Permits can be obtained online or through registered tour operators.

Altitude Sickness

Acclimatize for 48 hours in Leh before heading to higher areas.
Stay hydrated, avoid alcohol and strenuous activity initially.
Consider Diamox medication after consulting your doctor.

Getting Around

Hire bikes (Royal Enfield recommended) or cars with drivers.
Shared taxis available for popular routes.
Walking is the best way to explore Leh town.
