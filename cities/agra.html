The City of Taj

Agra, a city on the banks of the Yamuna river in Uttar Pradesh, is home to one of the Seven Wonders of the World - the magnificent Taj Mahal.

Historical Significance

Agra was the capital of the Mughal Empire from 1526 to 1658 and remains a major tourist destination due to its many Mughal-era buildings.

Cultural Heritage

Agra's culture has been influenced by Mughal traditions, with a rich legacy of art, music, and cuisine that continues to thrive today.

Modern Agra

While preserving its historical treasures, Agra has developed into a modern city with excellent infrastructure for tourists.

Travel Tips

Make the most of your visit to Agra with these helpful tips

Best Time to Visit

October to March when the weather is pleasant with temperatures between 8°C to 25°C. Avoid the summer months (April-June) when temperatures can exceed 45°C.

Getting There

Agra is well-connected by air, rail and road. The nearest airport is in Delhi (200km away). Agra has three main railway stations: Agra Cantt, Agra Fort, and Raja Ki Mandi.

Taj Mahal Visiting Hours

Open from sunrise to sunset (typically 6am-6:30pm) every day except Fridays. Night viewing is available on full moon nights and two days before and after.

Local Cuisine

Don't miss Agra's famous petha (a sweet made from ash gourd), Mughlai cuisine, and bedai with aloo sabzi (a popular breakfast dish).
