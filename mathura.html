The Holy City

Mathura, located on the banks of the Yamuna river in Uttar Pradesh, is revered as the birthplace of Lord Krishna and is one of the seven sacred cities of Hinduism.

Spiritual Significance

Mathura is one of the Sapta Puri (seven holy cities) of Hinduism and the birthplace of Lord Krishna. It attracts millions of pilgrims annually.

Rich Heritage

With a history spanning over 2,500 years, Mathura has been a cultural and religious center since ancient times, mentioned in the Ramayana and Mahabharata.

Festivals & Culture

Mathura is famous for its vibrant celebrations of Holi, Janmashtami, and other festivals that bring the city to life with colors, music, and dance.

Sacred Sites

Experience Holi in Mathura

Witness the birthplace of Holi celebrations! Mathura hosts the most vibrant and traditional Holi festivities in India, lasting for over a week with unique celebrations at different temples each day.

Join the Lathmar Holi in Barsana, the Flower Holi in Vrindavan, and the colorful Phoolon wali Holi at Banke Bihari Temple.

Travel Tips

Make the most of your spiritual journey to Mathura

Best Time to Visit

October to March offers pleasant weather (15C to 30C). For festival experiences, visit during Janmashtami (August/September) or Holi (March). Avoid summer months (April-June) with temperatures up to 45C.

Getting There

Mathura Junction is well-connected by rail to major cities. The nearest airport is in Delhi (160km). Agra (60km) also has an airport. Buses and taxis connect Mathura to nearby cities.

Temple Etiquette

Dress modestly (covered shoulders and knees), remove footwear before entering temples, and avoid photography in inner sanctums. Many temples have strict security checks.

Local Cuisine

Mathura is strictly vegetarian. Don't miss Mathura Peda, Kachori Sabzi, and the famous Chaat at Holi Gate. Try the sweets at Brijwasi Mithai Wala.
