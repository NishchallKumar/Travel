The Pink City

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is known as the Pink City due to its distinctive terracotta-pink buildings that line the streets of the historic center, painted in 1876 to welcome Queen Victoria's husband, Prince Albert :cite[4].

Royal Heritage

Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, Jaipur was India's first planned city. Its royal palaces and forts reflect the grandeur of Rajput architecture :cite[4].

Cultural Hub

Jaipur is a vibrant center for traditional arts, crafts, and cuisine. From intricate block printing to gemstone cutting, the city preserves centuries-old techniques :cite[2].

Modern Renaissance

While preserving its heritage, Jaipur has developed a modern cafe culture and luxury hospitality scene, blending tradition with contemporary comforts :cite[5].

Travel Tips

Essential information for your Jaipur visit

Best Time to Visit

November to February offers pleasant weather (8-25°C). Avoid April-June when temperatures exceed 40°C. Monsoon season (July-September) brings moderate rainfall :cite[4].

Getting Around

Use Uber/Ola for hassle-free transportation. Auto-rickshaws are plentiful - negotiate fares beforehand. For a unique experience, try cycle rickshaws in the old city.

Cultural Etiquette

Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites. Remove shoes before entering temples. Ask permission before photographing people :cite[4].

Shopping Tips

Bargain at local markets but fixed prices at government emporiums. Famous buys include blue pottery, block-printed textiles, gemstones, and Mojari footwear :cite[2].
